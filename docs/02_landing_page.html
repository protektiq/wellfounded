§ 01 · The work

An asylum case is, on average, forty hours of paperwork.

A single affirmative asylum filing requires a country conditions memo synthesized from dozens of authoritative sources, a client declaration drafted from interview notes in a language you may not speak, an evidence packet assembled from phone photos and WhatsApp screenshots, and a careful pass to ensure none of it contradicts the credible fear interview transcript on file.

Solo immigration attorneys file three or four asylum cases a year because that is the limit of what one person can do well. Legal aid organizations turn away clients who have viable claims because their attorneys are saturated. The work that decides whether a family can stay or must return is bottlenecked on the same forty hours, case after case.

Wellfounded compresses those forty hours into four. The case is still yours. The judgment is still yours. The drafting is no longer the bottleneck.

§ 02 · The workbench

Four surfaces, one case file.

Each component is useful on its own. Together they share a single case file, so the work compounds: the declaration informs the credibility audit, the audit checks the evidence, the evidence is matched to the country conditions, and nothing falls between the cracks.

01

Country Conditions Memo Generator

Specify the country, the basis of claim, and the timeframe. Wellfounded produces a memo grounded in State Department, USCIRF, UNHCR, Human Rights Watch, Amnesty International, and peer-reviewed academic sources — structured around the legal elements you need: general conditions, treatment of the relevant group, state actor involvement, internal relocation analysis.

Every paragraph cites a source. No paragraph is invented.

02

Declaration Drafter

Record or upload your client interview in their language. Wellfounded transcribes, translates, and produces a first-draft declaration in the client's voice — structured around the five protected grounds, the nexus requirement, and the elements your circuit requires.

Inconsistencies, gaps, and questions you should resolve with your client are flagged inline, never silently smoothed over.

03

Evidence Packet Assembler

Drop in everything your client has given you — phone photos, WhatsApp screenshots, medical records, school transcripts, news articles in other languages, scanned police reports. Wellfounded organizes, summarizes, and indexes them, matching each exhibit to the elements of the claim it corroborates.

Documents needing certified translation are flagged. Duplicates are merged. The exhibit list comes out filing-ready.

04

Credibility & Consistency Auditor

The keystone. Cross-checks the declaration, the I-589, prior statements available to you (credible fear interviews, airport statements, earlier filings), the evidence packet, and the country conditions memo for inconsistencies an asylum officer or immigration judge will flag.

Each inconsistency comes with a suggested resolution: clarify in the declaration, address in a pre-hearing brief, prepare client testimony on this point, or supplement with corroborating evidence.

§ 05 · Common questions

The questions every attorney asks first.

Does Wellfounded file the I-589 for me?

No. Wellfounded produces drafts. You review, revise, and sign every document. The attorney is always the final author. This is by design — both ethically and as a matter of law.

How do you handle hallucinations?

Every citation in a country conditions memo links to a verifiable source. The credibility auditor exists specifically to catch errors before filing. We publish our internal accuracy benchmarks and re-run them on every model release.

What about attorney-client privilege?

SOC 2 Type II. Zero-retention contracts with all model providers. Encryption at rest with per-tenant keys. We never train on your case data. Data destruction on demand.

Which languages do you support for declarations?

At launch: Spanish, Mandarin, French, Haitian Creole, Tigrinya, Dari, Pashto, Russian, Arabic, Portuguese, Amharic, and Ukrainian. We add languages by client demand and translator availability.

Will this work for defensive cases in immigration court?

The core workbench transfers cleanly. Removal defense and EOIR-specific workflows ship in early 2027 as part of our adjacent practice expansion.

How is this different from Harvey or ChatGPT?

Harvey is built for BigLaw at $1,200 per seat. ChatGPT is general-purpose and unsafe for filings. Wellfounded is built specifically for asylum practice, with a structured case file, citation enforcement, and a workflow that maps to how asylum cases are actually prepared.
